# Day 9 Reflection — LSTM, GRU & RNN Variants
Au Yi Teng (GHC)

## Summary
A deep dive into the evolution of Recurrent Neural Networks to solve the "vanishing gradient" problem.

## What I Learned
- LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory): The architecture of gates (Forget, Input, Output) to maintain long-term dependencies.
- GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit): A streamlined, more computationally efficient version of LSTM.
- Sequence Modeling: Determining when to use traditional RNNs versus gated variants.

## Reflection
Learning about the "Cell State" in LSTMs made it much clearer why standard RNNs struggle with long sentences. It feels like giving the neural network a selective memory.

## Challenges

- Understanding the trade-offs between GRU training speed and LSTM complexity.
