## 2) AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning

### What I learnt

The wrap-up lecture revisited the relationship between the key terms:

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is the broad goal of making machines act intelligently.
- **Machine Learning (ML)** is one way to achieve AI by learning from data.
- **Deep Learning (DL)** is a major branch of ML based on neural networks.
- **Machine Intelligence** focuses on building systems that can solve tasks intelligently using data, algorithms, and computing power.

A simple way to remember:

```text
AI is the goal.
ML is one method.
DL is one powerful ML approach.
Big data and compute help make modern AI work.
```

## 4) AI Challenges and Risks

### What I learnt

The lecture discussed several important AI risks.

### Job displacement

AI may replace or reduce demand for some types of work, such as:

- Customer service call centers
- Security monitoring
- Sorting and logistics work
- Dock work
- Taxi and truck driving

### Decision risk

AI systems may make wrong or harmful decisions, especially in high-stakes areas such as:

- Autonomous driving
- Insurance pricing
- Hiring and resume screening
- Security access control

### Fairness

Machine learning learns from data. If the data contains bias, the model may also learn and repeat that bias.

Example:

```text
Biased historical hiring data → biased resume screening model
```

### Explainability

Some deep learning models are difficult to interpret. This creates problems when users need to understand why a decision was made.

### Privacy and security

AI models often need large amounts of data. If the data is sensitive, privacy protection and security become important.

## 5) Data Problems

### What I learnt

A major theme was that data can mislead AI.

Important data problems:

- **Data bias**: the dataset does not fairly represent the real world.
- **Class imbalance**: some categories appear much more often than others.
- **Survivorship bias**: only looking at successful or surviving cases gives the wrong conclusion.
- **Data misuse**: using data without understanding how it was generated.
- **Feedback loop**: model decisions can influence future data collection.

A useful reminder:

```text
Bad data can produce confident but wrong AI results.
```

This is why AI work must include data understanding, not only model training.

## 7) Responsible AI

### What I learnt

Responsible AI means building AI systems in a way that is useful, fair, safe, and beneficial to people.

Important principles:

- **Fairness**: avoid unfair treatment across groups.
- **Privacy**: protect sensitive data.
- **Security**: prevent misuse and attacks.
- **Explainability**: help people understand AI decisions.
- **Human benefit**: AI should help people and society.

The lecture used Einstein's idea as a reminder: technology should care about people, not only technical performance.
